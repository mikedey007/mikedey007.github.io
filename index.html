Methodology

Objectives

Our goal is to empower travelers with the information of the risk of flight delays that would have an impact on their schedule. With this information, travelers can make better-informed decisions. Surveying existing tools, we found that none incorporates a combination of our three best innovations: interactive map, filtering capabilities, 10-day forecast.

Data and Machine Learning

Using six years of flight and weather data in 25 cities in the US, we’re able to train a machine-learned model based on random forests algorithm with upsampling. The learning problem was a binary classification and was separated between no delay and delay greater than 15 minutes. This model gives us a prediction accuracy of 79%.

Risk value

By combining user inputs and flight and weather data APIs, we’re able to load the features into the model and receive a predicted risk of delay as a percentage. In the case of layover cities are involved, the risk value would then be recalculated to compound the risk of delays in either leg of the journey. The 0-100% risk value makes it intuitive and easy to understand.

Limitation

The forecast is limited to 10 days in advance due to the limited weather API capability. In future work, there’s a possibility to forecast weather using historical weather data from the region and estimate the weather patterns further in the future.
Due to the increased cost of obtaining flight details through the flight API, we require that the users enter the airlines, origin and destination airports. There’s a possibility of integration between our tool and traveling sites that would have a more efficient approach to deliver the information to travelers.
